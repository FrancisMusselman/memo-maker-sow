Option 2: Design Lead and Product Continuity

A detail page for the Memo Maker SOW.

What this is

Upland takes the Memo Maker codebase out of Lovable and deploys it on infrastructure the studio owns. A senior engineer on the Upland side handles the port.

I lead Design and Product Continuity. The goal is to recreate the interface and, more importantly, make sure the rebuilt version preserves the logic, interaction model, and quality of what's already working end-to-end once it's live.

$11,000 flat across a 6-week build.

What's included

Week 1. Kickoff and handoff. A working session with the engineer walking through the app screen by screen. States, permissions, edge cases, and publishing behavior. Follow-up includes a written product brief covering core flows, the permission structure, and non-negotiables (row-level security at the database layer, signed URL expiry on image assets, dark mode parity, password gate behavior). Engineer receives read access to the Lovable project and the Supabase schema.

Weeks 2 through 5. Build support. One hour-long session per week to review progress, answer questions, and keep decisions moving. Slack availability in between for real-time clarification as implementation unfolds. Ongoing staging review as screens come online, flagging anything that drifts from the original intent or introduces inconsistencies.

I'm the decision-maker for UX and product questions about how the existing system should behave. Engineering decisions stay with engineering.

Week 6. QA and launch. Full walkthrough of the staging build. A focused punch list covering fidelity issues, spacing, copy, and interaction details. Final review and sign-off once the product reflects the intended experience.

What's not included

Net-new module design. If the team wants functionality beyond what exists in the current prototype (video embeds, chart blocks, timeline components, or extensions to the memo section system), that's a separate scope. It's better to let the ported version run for a few weeks before deciding what's actually needed.

Also not included: post-launch bug fixes beyond two weeks, ongoing hosting or maintenance, or changes to the Supabase schema or authentication model.

How scope changes work

If new requirements come up during the build, we pause and scope them as a change order. Nothing is absorbed into the flat fee without alignment first.

My role covers product and design decisions related to the existing system. Technical architecture decisions (stack choices, database changes, auth model) sit with engineering. Where the two overlap, we decide together.

What Upland provides

A senior engineer with roughly 40 to 60 hours allocated to the port. Infrastructure for deployment. Access to the team for the kickoff walkthrough and any mid-build decisions that require input.

Timeline

Six weeks from kickoff to launch. Flexible if the engineering timeline needs to adjust. The schedule assumes approximately 8 to 10 engineering hours per week and 5 to 8 hours per week on my side.

If the engineer can work faster, so can I. We will need to schedule a meeting to review the current build and talk details on process.

What happens after

The studio owns the application and can run independently. I'm available for questions or minor adjustments in the first two weeks after launch at no additional cost.

If the team decides to extend the tool (new modules, workflows, or a more fully developed system), that can be scoped once the product has been in use and real needs are clear.
